Visual 2-Hospital Beds vs COVID Mortality

The aim of this project was to present the data found from some preliminary COVID data. We have examined, the median age, the healthcare expenditure per capita and hospital beds per 1000 people and their correlation to the number of confirmed coronavirus cases and the number of deaths. It seems that that the there is a stronger correlation between quality of healthcare (as judged by the number of hospital beds and health care expenditure) and the survival rate from the virus than there is between median age and mortality.
